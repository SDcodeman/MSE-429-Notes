points (configurations of the manipulator) are defined and saved. Then the robot
is asked to move sequentially through all the points.

       Trajectory Generation

The basic problem of trajectory generation is how to move the manipulator from
an initial to a final position and orientation.
A trajectory describes the desired motion of the manipulator. This is position,
velocity and acceleration of each degree of freedom.




                             (Trajectory Generation, Craig 2003)

        Types of Motion

There are two types of motion between two desired configurations:


Point-to-point (PTP): This trajectory is based on the joint variables. Each
joint moves independently from each other, therefore the resulting end-effector
path is not controlled.
Continuous path (CP): The goal is to move the end-effector linearly along a
particular path.




        (Kuka’s Motion Types, the tool centre point (TCP) can connect two points moving PTP or CP)

        Point-to-point Motion

In point-to-point (PTP) motion, each joint moves independently from each other,
consequently the path followed is a function of the joint displacements.
Since the desired path is usually established in terms of the end-effector space
variables, the inverse kinematics is applied to describe the path in terms of the
joint variables.

        Continuous Path (CP) Motion

There are applications in which the end-effector must follow a straight line, like
in arc welding, spray painting, etc., in such cases continuous path motion is
employed.
In continuous path motion (CP) motion, joints have to work together to
accomplish the desired path.


A continuous path can be generated following two different methods
•   Via Points Scheme (Joint Space)
•   Cartesian Scheme (Task Space)




                  MSE 429 – Advanced Kinematics for Robotics System             Ch7 - 25
        Via Points (CP)

generate the trajectory in the task space, 𝐓 → 𝐓 .

matrix maintains the properties of 𝐑.

vector x, y, z and the Euler angles α, β, and γ, or the parameters of the
equivalent angle/axis method or quaternions.
Since the joint variables are the input variables of a manipulator, then the
inverse kinematics and the inverse velocity/acceleration problems must be solved
