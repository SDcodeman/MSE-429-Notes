        2.9 Transformation of Free Vectors

Velocity and force vectors transform differently
than the position vectors.


Note: In mechanics, two vectors are equal if
they have the same dimensions, magnitude,
and direction. Equal vector could have different
line of actions.

Line vector refers to a vector that is dependent on its line of action, along with
direction and magnitude. Example: Force is a line vector.


Free vector refers to a vector that may be positions anywhere in space without
loss or change of meaning, provided that magnitude and direction are preserved.
Example: a pure moment vector is a free vector.
